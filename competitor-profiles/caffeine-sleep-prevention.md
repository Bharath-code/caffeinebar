## What It Actually Does

Caffeine is a lightweight macOS menu bar utility that prevents the system from entering sleep mode, dimming the screen, or starting the screensaver. When activated (clicking the menu bar icon), it instructs macOS to stay awake. It serves one function: **keep the Mac awake**.

It is **not** a caffeine or coffee tracker. It has nothing to do with beverage consumption, health tracking, or coffee logging. The name "Caffeine" is metaphorical — it keeps the Mac "awake" like caffeine keeps a person awake.

## The Naming Confusion Problem

This is the most significant competitor in the brand confusion sense, not the product feature sense:

### Scale of the problem
- **"Caffeine" is the #1 search term** for anyone looking for a "caffeine app" on Mac — but they're finding a sleep-prevention utility, not a coffee tracker
- **Category overlap**: Both Caffeine and CaffeineBar are "menu bar apps" — users search by the category descriptor, making confusion inevitable
- **Historical weight**: Caffeine has existed for ~20 years. It's deeply entrenched in Mac user vocabulary.

## The Competitive Landscape of Sleep-Prevention Apps

The "keep Mac awake" category is crowded with modern alternatives that have surpassed the original Caffeine:

- **Amphetamine**: Currently the leading free utility, available on Mac App Store with hundreds of thousands of downloads and top-tier ratings. Advanced features: triggers, schedules, app-based rules.
- **Caffeinated** (caffeinated.app): Modern polished alternative with scheduling and advanced settings. Paid.
- **Theine** (theine.app): Sleek design, modern implementation, growing user base.

## Implications for CaffeineBar

### The challenge
1. **SEO difficulty**: Ranking for "caffeine" or "caffeine app" or "caffeine Mac" is nearly impossible because the sleep-prevention app owns those terms historically
2. **User confusion**: A user who downloads CaffeineBar expecting the sleep-prevention tool will be confused; a user who downloads the sleep-prevention tool expecting CaffeineBar's coffee tracking will also be confused
3. **Brand search dilution**: Of every 100 users who search "caffeine mac app," many are looking for the sleep utility, not CaffeineBar

### The mitigation (from the launch spec)
- **Hero copy gate**: CaffeineBar's landing page must include "coffee cup counter" in the first sentence to immediately signal what the product is
- **About page**: A dedicated page explicitly differentiating CaffeineBar from the Caffeine sleep-prevention app
- **PH tagline gate**: Product Hunt tagline must lead with the ambulance behavior, not "caffeine tracker"
- **Anti-ICP positioning**: No "wellness" or "sleep prevention" language that could compound confusion

### The opportunity
- **Only coffee tracker with a Mac icon**: No caffeine/coffee tracker has a macOS menu bar presence with a distinctive icon. The skull-and-crossbones icon is visually distinct from Caffeine's coffee cup icon.
- **Category creation**: CaffeineBar can own "coffee cup counter" as a search term — it's unambiguous and no competitor uses it
- **The joke is the differentiator**: If CaffeineBar leads with "your Mac calls an ambulance at cup 5," no one will confuse it with a sleep-prevention utility
